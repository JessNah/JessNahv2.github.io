Hack The North

My first hackathon

One of the best experiences of my life, the entire time from start to finish, the volunteers and people working there went out of their way to be positive, fun, motivating and just plain cheerful and it was great. When you went to get your food, there'd be music and people dancing and what a blast it was. You don't realise how incredible a good atmosphere is, until you've been up 2 days and nights in a row, programming. My team was amazing, we had a blast together and kept each other going. We created an app named AICOG, which was basically a meddlesome alarm clock that made switching it off without being cognitively awake very difficult. You had to answer questions, solve puzzles, or play a mini game that required quick reflexes and concentration, in order to turn off the very annoying alarm ring tones. We had a lot of fun creating it. There was an incredible variety of workshops there to learn from, so much so, that we ended up modifying our app to incoorporate a pebble watch feature after attending a code with pebble workshop. All the speaches, were exceptionally inspirational and funny. The highlight being, the CEO of reddit's speech.

My favorite part of the experience was definitely the entire atmosphere. Something about being surrounded by fellow coders and tech-enthusiasts just feels very homey. (Though collecting up a loot bag was pretty fun too, I won't lie.)
